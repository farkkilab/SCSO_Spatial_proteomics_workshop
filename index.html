Spatial Proteomics: From Pixels to Phenotypes#

Welcome to the Spatial Proteomics Workshop (2026). In this session, we move beyond simple microscopy to explore high-dimensional tissue imaging. You will learn to navigate multiplexed data, manage technical image parameters, and translate raw fluorescence into a spatial atlas of the tumor microenvironment.

Important

Software Check: We will use Avivator and the CycIF Viewer, which run directly in your web browser for initial visual exploration. For the computational analysis, we will use Scimap and UTAG within a Python/Jupyter environment.

Learning Objectives#

By the end of this workshop, you will be able to:

  • Evaluate Image Channels: Explain what specific biological signals represent in a multiplexed stack.

  • Master Visualization: Adjust contrast, intensity ranges, and colormaps to highlight specific ROI (Regions of Interest).

  • Assess Data Quality: Identify bit depth, pixel-to-micron scales, and the impact of signal aggregation.

  • Identify Phenotypes: Relate staining patterns to cell morphology and distinguish between lineage and functional markers.

  • Measure Social Distance of Cells: Quantify the spatial proximity between immune cells and tumor nests to assess infiltration.

  • Map Recurrent Cellular Neighbourhoods (RCNs): Use k-means clustering to identify the Tumor-Stromal Interface (TSI).

  • Automate Domain Discovery: Apply UTAG to identify unsupervised architectural neighborhoods across different samples.
